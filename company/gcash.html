In the Global South, the everyday account is the wallet that reached the customer first, not the branch that never arrived.

In the Philippines, the account people actually use is not a bank.

GCash grew from a way to buy phone load into the country's everyday account: around 81 million monthly active users, more than 94 million Filipinos who have used it, and some 2.5 million sellers and merchants taking payment, from the mall to the sari-sari store. Across thousands of islands, the phone reached where the branch never opened a door.

The decision that mattered: build the whole account into one app, for the customer the branch skipped.
