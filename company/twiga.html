The biggest market in the city was the one no formal supply chain served.

Nairobi, 2014. Peter Njonjo left a multinational's regional business to build for the mama mboga - the kiosk vendor feeding the city from some 180,000 informal outlets with no reliable supply chain. Twiga grew to serve around 140,000 retail outlets, sourcing from more than 17,000 farmers and cutting post-harvest losses from roughly 30 percent to four.

The founding decision that mattered: treat the informal market as the market.
